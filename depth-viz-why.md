# Why the depth visualization change

The depth head outputs metric depth (meters), and the visualizer previously colorized it one fixed way — INFERNO with linear min-max normalization — which is fine for reading absolute distance but a poor visual map of a scene: a linear min-to-max map spends most of its color range on the two extreme pixels, so a few far-background or sky pixels stretch the range and collapse everything in the useful mid-ground (people, nearby objects) into a narrow band of near-identical color, and near=dark / far=bright is the opposite of how people expect depth to read. This change adds two opt-in options (defaults unchanged, zero regression) that together give the DepthAnything-style look: disparity mode colorizes inverse depth (1/d) clipped to the 2nd-98th percentile, which expands the near field where detail matters, compresses the far field, and stops outlier pixels from stealing the range (recovering the fine detail INFERNO was flattening); and a Spectral colormap (a baked Spectral_r LUT, no matplotlib at render time) maps near=warm and far=cool for the intuitive direction. Metric + inferno stay the defaults for true meters and cross-frame consistency, while the new modes are for visual inspection where they read much cleaner.
